Arena Specifications

  • The base of the arena is made up of white colored flex sheet with a black colored line (to be followed) on it, for autonomous line following.

  • The thickness of the line will be 3cm.

  • The course line may have any shape out of acute, obtuse and right angle curves. So design a dynamic line follower.

  • There will be a building boxes (check points) fixed at starting and ending points on the arena. The robot should stop at the point where there is building, change it’s state automatically to manual, do the needful of picking up the small workers, rotate to detect the normal line following line and again as soon as it detects this line, it should autonomously change it’s state from manual to autonomous again and trace it’s path back to starting point and keep those workers in safe-boxes.

  • Organizers decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.

  • FARMTRACK : The track will be rough field track with sharp turns and twists. Both the speed and control agility of your bot is important if you choose for this track. The robot should be able to cross bumps, sandy track, slippery track and the rough patches on the track. 1) The width of the track will be around 1 foot for most of the part. 2) Despite the organizer’s best efforts to keep the quality of track intact, the track is subjected to undergo wear and tear as machines run over it. The machine is expected to perform on such weakened track.

Robot Specifications

  • The robot should fit inside a cube of 20-25 cm.

  • The robot can be both internally or externally powered.

  • The power supply at any point on robot should not be greater than 20V.

  • However incase of onboard power supply, the robot along with the power supply should fit in the cube of above mentioned size.

  • A tolerance of 5% is allowed in the dimensions.

  • A mechanism to pick up the worker to be included solely on the robot body.

  • Control: Wired/Wireless.

  • For wireless extra points(50 points) will be awarded.

  • Min. Length of Wire(for manual wired bot): 1.5m.

Team Specifications

  • A team can consist of maximum 4 members. Individuals may also participate.

Rules

  • Event Managers and Coordinators reserve the right to ask for explanation about the robot at any time during the event.

  • Winner of competition is the one who successfully collects workers to and from.

  • The robot should follow the line accurately. If the robot deviates from the line, contestants are allowed to keep the bot manually in its right direction {HAND TOUCH would account for deducting of points}.

  • Only two restarts are allowed in each round(with the negative points embarked upon).

  • A sample arena will be available for calibration and the final arena will be disclosed only after starting the rounds.

  • Points for different traps of manual or autonomous arena would vary according to their difficulty level, i.e., V Shape or 90 degree turn in autonomous and Net walk , Long Bridge or Zig Zag Bumper in Manual.

  • Between trails and events, participants should not feed information about the arena to the bot.

  • The judgment of organizers is final and abiding to all.

  • We reserve the right at any time and in our sole discretion to make changes to rules and regulations without prior notice.

  • In case of any disputes the organizers decision will be final and binding.

  • The participants are requested not to assume anything without conforming it from the event Organizers.

Scoring

  • The robot should fit inside a cube of 20-25 cm.

  • Scoring will vary and the same shall be communicated to the contestants before the start of each round.

  • The Evaluation sheet for scoring and relevant rules pertaining to that would be announced before the event, on the day of event.
